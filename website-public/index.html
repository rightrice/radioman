The Problem

The wireless world around you
is invisible until you look.

Most people move through wireless environments every day without a clear picture of what's actually there. Radioman changes that.

Wireless is Invisible by Default

You can't see RF. Without the right tool in hand, you're guessing what networks, bands, and signals are actually present in any given space — home, office, venue, or anywhere in between.

Observations Don't Survive the Moment

You notice something — a weak signal zone, an unexpected SSID, a channel collision — and then you walk away and it's gone. Mental notes are lossy by design.

No Structure Means No Pattern

Without a consistent format for capturing what you find, you can't compare sessions, track changes over time, or build on what you already know. Curiosity deserves better than a notes app.

Building from Memory is Lossy

Reconstructing what you observed after the fact means losing detail, sequence, and spatial context. The right time to capture data is while you're standing in it.

Interference Hides in Plain Sight

Rogue devices, channel congestion, overlapping coverage, and unexpected SSIDs are everywhere. Without systematic discovery, they stay invisible — even to people who know to look.

Every New Environment Starts from Zero

Walking into a new space — a home, a building, a hotel, a lab — with no visibility into the wireless environment means starting blind. Every time. Radioman fixes that.

The System

One kit. Complete field visibility.

Radioman is a layered system — hardware, local software, and optional cloud sync — designed to slot into any workflow without getting in the way.

It works completely without an internet connection. XPLT sync is available when you want to tie field results into structured records, projects, and longer-running documentation.

Field Workflow

From first walkthrough
to structured field data.

Radioman fits into any site visit without adding friction. A complete session takes less time than reconstructing notes afterward — and produces something you can actually use.

01

Start a Session

Name the session and optionally tag it with a location or project. Radioman logs all activity against this session from the moment you start.

02

Walk the Site

Move through the building with Radioman active. Wireless networks are discovered and logged in real time as you move through different areas and floors.

03

Capture Observations

Add field notes as you go — dead zones, unexpected devices, coverage gaps, access point conditions. Notes are timestamped with the wireless context from that moment.

04

Review the Session

When the walkthrough is complete, review the session on the local dashboard. See everything that was observed, flagged, and noted during the visit.

05

Export or Sync

Export the session locally in a structured format, or optionally sync selected results into your XPLT workspace to build records across locations, projects, and time.

XPLT Integration

Radioman collects.
XPLT organizes.

Radioman is designed to work without an account or internet connection. When you want to organize field results across multiple sessions, locations, or projects, XPLT is the optional sync destination.

Selected session data can be saved into your XPLT workspace and organized by location, project, and survey. XPLT stores the structured records you need to track changes over time, compare environments, and build a personal wireless knowledge base.

"Radioman captures what's in the air. XPLT turns selected sessions into structured records you can actually use."

Sync is always controlled — you decide what gets saved and when.

Use Cases

For anyone who wants to understand
the signal around them.

01

Home Network Mapping

Walk your home and build a real picture of your wireless environment — dead zones, channel congestion, signal overlap, and everything your router's admin panel never tells you.

02

Home Lab Documentation

Document the wireless footprint of your lab as it grows. Track what's running, where signals reach, and how your setup changes over time with structured session data.

03

Wireless Site Surveys

Walk any space and build a signal coverage picture from actual observation. Great for pre-deployment planning, validating AP placement, or understanding an environment you didn't set up.

04

Security Research & Assessment

Conduct structured wireless discovery during authorized assessments. Capture what's visible, what's unexpected, and what warrants a closer look — without relying on memory or informal notes.

05

Interference Hunting

Track down the source of slow speeds, dropped connections, or unexplained signal problems. Systematic wireless visibility makes interference problems findable instead of just frustrating.

06

Event & Venue Awareness

Walk a conference, venue, hotel, or public space and see what's broadcasting. Understand the wireless density before you connect — or just because you're curious what's out there.

07

Network Documentation

Build a baseline wireless record for any environment you own or manage. A single Radioman session produces more structured data than hours of manual notes or scattered screenshots.

08

Personal Knowledge Projects

Build a record of the wireless environments you care about over time. Compare sessions, track changes, and sync selected data to XPLT to keep a living wireless knowledge base.
